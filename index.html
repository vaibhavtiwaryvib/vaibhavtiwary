About Me

A dedicated researcher focused on aging biology who explores how mitochondrial signaling, epigenetic regulation, and cellular stress responses shape lifespan and healthspan, with a particular interest in plant-derived compounds as potential longevity drugs and lifespan-extending interventions in model organisms.

Research Philosophy

My research focuses on the intersection of epigenetics, mitochondrial biology, and aging. I believe that understanding how cellular stress responses are inherited across generations will unlock new strategies for promoting healthy aging. This work also considers plant-derived longevity compounds within translational frameworks.

Currently pursuing my PhD at the University of Rostock and Leibniz Institute of Farm Animal Biology (FBN), I investigate how light-activated proton pumps can modulate cellular energetics and influence lifespan in Drosophila melanogaster.
