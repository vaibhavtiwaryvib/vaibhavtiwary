About Me

I am a PhD Researcher currently finishing my thesis at the Shahaf Peleg lab (FBN Dummerstorf & University of Rostock). My research lies at the intersection of metabolism, epigenetics, and aging biology.

My doctoral work has focused on two primary avenues: using light-activated proton pumps to reprogram mitochondrial metabolism (bypassing the ETC) and investigating the pharmacological effects of Reserpine on longevity.

"Understanding the fundamental mechanisms of aging to extend healthspan through metabolic and epigenetic interventions."

Research Highlights

Exploring mitochondrial signaling, optogenetics, and pharmacological interventions in Drosophila melanogaster.

Optogenetic Reprogramming

Characterizing light-activated proton pumps to partially replace ATP production in mitochondria, bypassing the electron transport chain to modulate aging.

Pharmacology of Aging

Investigating Reserpine (monoamine depletion) for lifespan extension in Drosophila, balancing longevity benefits with stress resilience trade-offs.

Epigenetic Inheritance

Studying Transgenerational Epigenetic Inheritance (TEI) via the male germline, focusing on PcG/TrxG proteins and heat-stress induced memory.
