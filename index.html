About Me

A passionate scientist dedicated to unraveling the molecular mysteries of aging.

Research Philosophy

My work is driven by the belief that understanding the fundamental mechanisms of aging can lead to interventions that extend healthspan. I study the interplay between genetics, epigenetics, metabolism, and environmental factors, with a focus on Transgenerational Epigenetic Inheritance (TEI) — how an organism's experiences shape the traits of its descendants.

At FBN Dummerstorf in Shahaf Peleg’s lab and as a PhD Student at University of Rostock, I use Drosophila melanogaster to combine optogenetics with metabolic and mitochondrial assays, exploring how light-controlled mitochondrial modulation and aging-related drugs influence lifespan and behavior.

Research Projects

A summary of my key research projects throughout my academic career.

PhD Research: Optogenetics in Aging

Characterizing light-activated proton pumps to modulate mitochondrial function and lifespan in Drosophila.

PhD Research: Pharmacology of Aging

Investigating the effects of the neuromodulator reserpine on longevity and its trade-offs with stress resilience.

Master's Thesis: Epigenetic Inheritance

Investigated PcG/TrxG proteins in transgenerational inheritance of heat-stress induced epigenetic memory.

Cancer & Phytochemicals

Studied the cytotoxic effect of medicinal plant fractions against MCF-7 breast cancer cells.

Longevity & Gene Expression

Analyzed gene expression in lifespan extension of Drosophila treated with ellagic acid.

Developmental Toxicology

Examined the effect of Cobalt Chloride on the development of Zebrafish embryos.

Genetic Polymorphism in CKD

Analyzed the XRCC1 Arg399Gln polymorphism in Chronic Kidney Disease patients.
